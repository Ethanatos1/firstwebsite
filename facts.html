Top 3 Facts about New Zealand

1. New Zealand is home to the world's southernmost capital city, Wellington.

Wellington is located at the southern tip of the North Island and is known for its vibrant culture, beautiful harbors, and stunning natural surroundings. Being located at 41.3°S, it is the southernmost capital city in the world.

2. New Zealand has more sheep than people.

With over 23.6 million sheep nationwide, there are over 4.5 sheep for every person in the country. This means we have more sheep than the number of people in Beijing!

3. New Zealand was the first ever country to allow women to vote in parliamentary elections.

In 1893, New Zealand became the first country in the world to grant women the right to vote in parliamentary elections. This act was signed by Governer Lord Glasgow, with the movement led by Kate Sheppard (who now appears on the New Zealand $10 note).
