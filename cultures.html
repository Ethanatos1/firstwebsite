Top 3 Cultural celebrations in New Zealand

1. Matariki (Māori New Year)

Matariki is the Māori New Year, celebrated when the Pleiades star cluster rises in the dawn sky. It's a time for reflection, remembrance, and celebration of the upcoming year.

2. Waitangi Day

Waitangi Day is celebrated on February 6th and commemorates the signing of the Treaty of Waitangi in 1840 between the British Crown and Māori chiefs. It's a day of reflection on New Zealand's history and the relationship between Māori and the Crown.

3. Pasifika Festival

The Pasifika Festival is a vibrant celebration of Pacific Island cultures, featuring traditional music, dance, food, and art. It showcases the rich diversity of Pacific Island communities in New Zealand.
